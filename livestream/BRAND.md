# Cabin Hours — brand playbook

> Cozy lo-fi to study, work & sleep. **@cabinhours**
>
> Scope note: this brand and playbook apply **only to the `livestream/` module**
> (the always-on / weekly background-loop channel). It does not touch the main
> Shorts pipeline or anything outside `livestream/`.

Cabin Hours is run less like a "music channel" and more like a small cozy radio
station + visual-IP studio: a recognizable world, a steady listening habit, and
a rights-clean catalog underneath. Condensed from the strategy research; this is
the operating version we actually build against.

## Positioning

- **One promise:** a warm, human-made place to focus, work, and wind down.
- **Visual anchor (the IP):** a warm-lit log cabin by a frozen skating pond —
  snow, string lights, woodsmoke, aurora. We vary *time of day, weather, season,
  and props*, never the core world. (Rendered, not stock footage — a deliberate
  human-made / no-stock signal.)
- **Contexts we serve:** study/focus, remote work/coding, relax/unwind, sleep,
  late-night. We brand by *context and world*, not by genre purity.

## Title formula

`[scene/world] + [listener task] + [sound palette]` — titles do three jobs at
once (describe the world, the task, and the sound). Titles and descriptions
matter far more than tags for discovery, so lead with the descriptive words.
Each world ships this metadata in the build manifest (`title`, `task`,
`palette`, `playlists`) ready to paste into the upload.

## Playlists = products (not shelves)

`Focus` · `Sleep` · `Work` · `Night Drive` · `Rainy Café` · `Morning` ·
`Chill` · `Golden Hour` · `Holiday`. Each world feeds 2–3 of these. Name them
for the *listener's task*, re-share them between uploads.

## Content lanes

1. **Recurring long-form mixes** (flagship; ~35 min–2 h) — the searchable mood
   product and test bed. *This loop generator powers these and the radio.*
2. **Original singles / EPs / compilations** — the rights-clean catalog for DSPs.
3. **Always-on / long live radio** — habit + community (add once stable).
4. **Ambience-led uploads** (sleep/rain, multi-hour) — low-friction repeat views.
5. **Short vertical cutdowns + community posts** — reminders, reach, personality.

## Rights model (keep it clean)

- Build from **original performances/sound design**, **royalty-free libraries**
  (Splice/Loopcloud/BandLab — commercial use OK), or **pre-cleared samples**
  (Tracklib). Never uncleared rips/flips.
- The **claimable assets are the individual tracks** you own — long curated mixes
  and continuous radio are *audience products*, ineligible as Content ID
  references. Register works with a PRO + mechanical collection.

## Monetization layers (ads are never the whole business)

YouTube ads/live → memberships → Patreon → Bandcamp/DSP catalog → merch →
brand/sync (only with a clean rights chain). Aggregate audience on YouTube,
monetize catalog on DSPs, deepen with direct support.

## KPI focus (in order)

Packaging (CTR ~3.5–6%) → retention (avg view duration rising) → loyalty
(casual→regular) → live engagement (peak concurrents + chat rate) →
monetization readiness (500 subs / 3k hrs, then 1k / 4k hrs).

## Build cadence

One flagship world-mix every 1–2 weeks · one original upload monthly · one
seasonal tentpole quarterly · playlists per context · live radio once the
catalog, visuals, and moderation are ready.
