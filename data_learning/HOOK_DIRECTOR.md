# THE HOOK DIRECTOR

The first frame and first line decide everything. ~71% of viewers choose to keep
watching inside the first 3 seconds; a video that loses them in the first second
is algorithmically suppressed no matter how good the rest is. The hook is a
promise the video pays off. This director designs and grades the opening ~3–8s:
the opening **visual** and the opening **line**.

The canonical death opener — the one this whole director exists to stop — is a
**calm establishing shot: a wide sky, a slow orbital drift, a cloud, a logo.**
No dominant subject, no motion, no tension. "Showing clouds is not interesting
in any way, even if relevant." Reject it every time.

## The hard-fail gates (any one → reject, redesign)

1. **CALM_OPENER** — first frame is a calm/ambient/ambiguous image with no
   dominant subject (sky, cloud, ocean, orbital drift, gradient).
2. **STATIC_FIRST_FRAME** — no motion in frame 1 and no push-in/zoom.
3. **LOGO_OR_TITLE** — opens on a logo, bumper, or title card before earning it.
4. **SUBJECT_NOT_LEGIBLE** — you can't tell what you're looking at in < 1s.
5. **THROAT_CLEARING** — first line is a greeting / "welcome back" / apology /
   engagement ask.
6. **CONTEXT_SETTING** — first line sets up rather than hits: "to understand X we
   first need…", "imagine…", "today we're going to…", the buried lede.
7. **NO_GAP_NO_STAKES** — first line states a fact that *closes* curiosity and
   carries no stakes, number, or bold claim (a caption, not a hook).
8. **HOOK_PAYOFF_MISMATCH** — the opening promise isn't what the video delivers.

## Visual score (0–5), need ≥3

+1 motion in frame 1 (or a fast push-in) ·
+1 one dominant, instantly-legible subject ·
+1 an anomaly / "wait-what" / prediction error ·
+1 a camera move OR quick-cut energy OR a 0.3s micro-preview of the payoff ·
+1 a bold on-screen text line (one line, readable < 1s — most viewers are sound-off).

The engine's objective proxy: the opening's **hook_appeal** (interest_judge) must
clear ~0.42. A grey-cloud open scores ~0.08 and auto-fails on APPEAL — that is the
CALM_OPENER gate in numbers. A designed graphic, a number-slam, a vivid or
high-contrast image scores high.

## Line score (0–5), need ≥3

+1 fires a curiosity void (states an effect, withholds the cause / open loop) ·
+1 stakes or threat (negativity bias: kills, deadliest, destroys, erases, never) ·
+1 a specific number / concrete noun ("10,000 nuclear bombs", not "a lot") ·
+1 self-relevance ("you/your") OR in-media-res immediacy ·
+1 stacks 2+ triggers in one sentence.

Proven line patterns (pick one, stack triggers):
- **Curiosity gap:** state the effect, withhold the cause.
- **Counter-intuitive claim:** assert something that violates expectation.
- **Stakes/threat:** name the danger up front.
- **"You're doing X wrong":** call out a held belief.
- **Number/specific:** a concrete figure = credibility + gap.
- **In-media-res:** drop into the middle of the action.

Specificity is the pro/amateur line: "23 pounds in 9 weeks" beats "weight fast".

## Pass condition

**No gate tripped AND visual ≥ 3 AND line ≥ 3 AND total ≥ 7/10.** Otherwise
redesign the hook — not polish the words, redesign the concept (new visual and/or
new line).

## Structure

The 0–3s **curiosity hook** opens the primary loop the whole video closes. A
**retention hook** at ~0:05–0:15 states the payoff/promise so the viewer commits.
Re-hook (new visual / mini-loop / graphic reveal) roughly every ~30s and at the
0:10–0:20 dip. One primary loop + 1–2 secondary; more = overload. The hook must be
honest to the content, and ideally the opening image recurs/resolves at the end
(return-to-opening; peak-end).

## When the footage is calm (the hurricane problem)

If the only footage is intrinsically low-appeal (orbital cloud), do NOT open on
it. Options, best-first:
- Open on a **designed high-appeal graphic** — a number-slam ("10,000 nuclear
  bombs / every second"), a bold on-screen text hook, or an animated diagram —
  then cut to the footage.
- Open on the single most **dynamic, legible** footage window (the eye pushed in
  fast), never the calm establishing wide.
- Lead with a **micro-preview** of the film's most striking moment.
